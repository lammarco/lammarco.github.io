About Me

My name is Marco Lam, currently studying both Computer Science (Specialization: Intelligent Systems) and Computer Game Science at UC Irvine. I am passionate in all things game-related, but especially in technical and design-related aspects.

Games and programming are massive parts of my life. Outside of school and spending time with friends & family, I am most likely developing games or programming side projects. Otherwise I am probably spending free time to play games.

Passion for Games

My early passion for games came from countless playthroughs of Pokemon and Megaman series on the DS. After that, I spent a few years playing mobile rhythm games, but am now on a spree with platformers (especially metroidvanias).

I love technical parts of games. This not only includes glitch-hunting and technical tricks, but also automating in technical games and optimizing stats. My favorite technical game is Minetest for its capacity as a mod engine. Of course, mesecon and pipeworks are a must (think: redstone builds in Minecraft).

Programming and Realizing Ideas

With so many rules and principles, programming can be a strict subject like math and science. Despite those restrictions, I find it to be a versatile tool for problem-solving and translating ideas into reality. Case in point: being able to develop my own games by breaking down my ideas into code. Recently, I also developed an interest for machine learning because it blends two subjects of my interests (programming and optimization).

A Non-technical Hobby

Outside of my technical interests, my main hobby is music; my favorite type is a mix of instrumental and electronic. Due to my rhythm game experiences, I prefer complex songs, whether it be complex in rhythm or tone. However, despite the genres I play, the Touhou series (EoSD, with exception of Byakuren Theme's transition to E minor) became my favorite game soundtrack with its vibrant and memorable melodies.
