Marco Lam

About Me

My name is Marco Lam, currently studying both Computer Science (Specialization: Intelligent Systems) and Computer Game Science at UC Irvine. I am passionate in all things game-related, but especially in technical and design-related aspects.

In fact, games make up a massive part of my life. Outside of school and chores, I spend almost the rest of my time into developing or playing games, as well as writing game-related programs. As such, programming and math are my favorite subjects due to their ability to model and verify ideas.

Passion for Games

My early passion for games came from countless playthroughs of Pokemon Emerald, while the GBA and DS Megaman series helped solidify my interest. After that, I spent a few years playing mobile rhythm games, but have now moved on to a spree on platformers (especially metroidvanias). Other than those genres, I enjoy technical games such as Minetest (mesecon and pipeworks)

I love glitch-hunting and technical tricks. Although my player skill is lacking, seeing people push the game to its limits is always entertaining; the technicalities underneath make the feats all the more amazing.

A Non-technical Hobby

Outside of my technical interests, my main hobby is music — my favorite type being a mix of instrumental and electronic. Due to my rhythm game experiences, I prefer the complex parts in songs, whether it be rhythm or tone. However, despite the genres I play, the Touhou series (EoSD, with exception of Byakuren Theme's transition to E minor) became my favorite game soundtrack with its vibrant and memorable melodies.

As someone who enjoys creating and learning, I also learned to compose music to a degree, and ended up as the audio designer for the College Game Jam game GremLink.
